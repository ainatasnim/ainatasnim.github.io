AIS KACANG

Ais Kacang or also known as Ais Batu Campur (ABC) is a popular dessert in Malaysia. Initially it was made from ground ice and mixed with red beans. Peanut ice was served with a variety of ingredients such as fruits that made it more delicious and colorful. It is also mixed with red beans, sweet corn, cincau, jelly, peanuts and ice cream. it is mixed with condensed milk, evaporated milk poured over a pile of ground ice along with a little rose syrup. There is also the Peanut Ice that is sprinkled with melted chocolate to add flavor.

CHAR KUEW TEOW

Char Kuew Teow is a very popular food that can be found in Malaysia. Usually it is fried with high heat with liquid soy sauce, thick soy sauce, chilli, shrimp paste, shrimp, mussels, bean sprouts and chives. In addition, Char Kuew Teow is also fried along with eggs, shrimp, mussels, bean sprouts and more. This dish is also served on banana leaves placed in a plate to further enhance the aroma.

SATAY

Satay is a slice of meat that is marinated, pricked with a skewer and roasted on a charcoal stove. Usually satay is served with peanut gravy and also sliced ​​onion and cucumber. There are various types of satay in Malaysia, namely meat satay, chicken satay, stomach satay and goat satay. It is a favorite of almost all Malaysians and can be easily found at hawker stalls, night markets and restaurants. This satay is also delicious to eat with nasi himpit which can be used as a rice substitute. It is also often used as a light meal in the evening and is very popular as one of the dishes ahead of the festive celebrations.

NASI LEMAK

Nasi Lemak is a Malay food dish. It is made from rice cooked with coconut milk, onion, ginger and pandan leaves. Nasi Lemak is also known as Malaysia's national dish and can be found in every corner of Malaysia. Nasi lemak in Malaysia usually contains sauteed sambal, fried anchovies, fried peanuts, cucumber and boiled eggs. There are also Nasi Lemak sellers who serve it with other side dishes such as squid sambal, kerang sambal, fried egg, fried chicken, rendang and so on.

ROJAK

Rojak is a salad that contains a variety of fruits and vegetables. Various types of rojak, namely fruit rojak, Indian rojak, Penang rojak and kangkung squid rojak. For rojak buah usually consists of pieces of cucumber, mango, pineapple, sengkuang, bean sprouts and also fried tofu. Rojak buah gravy is made from soy sauce, belacan, lime juice, chilli and ground peanuts. While the Indian rojak is known as pasembor or rojak mamak. This rojak gravy is a thick peanut gravy that tastes sweet and spicy. This rojak consists of potatoes, boiled eggs, bean sprouts, sengkuang, fried tofu, fried cucur, fried shrimp or squid.

MEE KARI

Mee Kari is a flavorful and unique dish from a combination of Chinese and Indian dishes. The broth is made from curry spices mixed with coconut milk, chilli paste, dried tofu, shrimp, chicken, squid, mussels, lemongrass and mint leaves. This dish has become a favorite of many regardless of race and is easily available at local restaurants. The delicious curry sauce can be eaten with noodles, kuetiaw or vermicelli and can be eaten with sour chillies or pickled chillies.

NASI KANDAR

Nasi kandar is a very popular dish and comes from Penang. It is a white rice dish served with curry sauce and additional side dishes. Usually it is eaten with fried chicken, goat curry, fried squid, fried shrimp, fried fish, beef and so on. It is also often complemented with vegetables such as fried bitter gourd, fried cabbage, side dishes and so on. Almost all communities are very fond of Nasi Kandar no matter where..

ROTI CANAI

Roti Canai is a type of Indian flat bread that can be found in most mamak shops in Malaysia. Roti Canai is also known as ‘flying bread’ due to the process of tossing and turning before cooking. Apart from that. Usually Roti Canai is served with dhal bean gravy, or various types of curries at mamak shops such as fish curry, chicken curry, goat curry and so on. It can also be served with granulated sugar for individuals who love sweet foods.There are many other Roti Canai options such as Egg Bread, Thin Tissue Bread, onion bread, onion egg bread, banana bread, planta bread which is often served with granulated sugar.

MAMAK FRIED MEE

Mee Goreng Mamak is another simple and delicious dish. Usually it can be found in Mamak restaurants as well as regular restaurants in Malaysia. Mamak Fried Noodles are usually mixed with slices of chicken or meat, chilli, mustard, bean sprouts, eggs, onions, tomatoes, soy sauce and so on. The dish was a bit dry but quite appetizing.

NASI DAGANG

Nasi Dagang is a Malaysian and Southern Thai food. It is served with steamed nasi lemak cooked with coconut milk and halba seeds, giving a unique aroma to the rice. Other ingredients to enjoy with rice are fish curry, boiled eggs, pickled vegetables and chicken or meat rendang. This food can be enjoyed along the East Coast of Peninsular Malaysia and also Southern Thailand.

ASAM LAKSA

Asam Laksa is a traditional dish of the Baba and Nyonya community were originally Chinese immigrants who came to Malaya. This dish is rice noodles served with spicy fish gravy. They have incorporated Malay culture applied in daily life. The combination of elements of these two cultures has resulted in this Asam Laksa dish. This dish is eaten with other ingredients such as sliced ​​cucumber, onion, chilli, pineapple, salad leaves, kesum leaves and kantan flowers. This white laksa noodle is eaten with gravy and also otaku dang paste to add a more delicious taste especially in Penang.

NASI kerabu

Nasi kerabu is a Malaysian cuisine rice dish, a type of nasi ulam, in which blue-colored rice is eaten with dried fish or fried chicken, crackers, pickles and other salads. The blue color of the rice comes from the petals of Clitoria ternatea (butterfly-pea) (bunga telang), as a natural food coloring in cooking it.The rice can also be cooked with plain white rice or using turmeric. It is often eaten with solok lada and is also eaten with keropok. Nasi kerabu is very popular in the east coast states of Peninsular Malaysia such as Kelantan and Terengganu.
